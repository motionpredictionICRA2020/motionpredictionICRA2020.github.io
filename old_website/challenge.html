Challenge

In this proposed workshop, we want to run a challenge on trajectory forecasting, referred to as our TrajNet++ challenge. The challenge will be running for the first time in the upcoming AMLD'20 conference in January 2020. It will potentially be its second edition in ICRA 2020. Trajectory forecasting of agents has two main challenges: interaction among agents and interaction with the scene. Hence, we will run two sub-challenges: (i) agent-agent, and (ii) agent-space.

  • Agent-Agent: In the past few years, several novel methods have been proposed to tackle agent-agent interactions. However, most methods have been evaluated on limited data. Furthermore, these methods have been evaluated on different subsets of the available data without proper indexing of trajectories making it difficult to objectively compare the forecasting techniques. In this agent-agent sub-challenge, we share new large scale training data. Researchers have to study how their method performs in explicit agent-agent scenarios. Our sub-challenge provides not only proper indexing of trajectories but also a unified extensive evaluation system to test the gathered methods for a fair comparison.
  • Agent-Space: To anticipate motion of each agent, scene should also be taken into account due to its strong constraint. Despite its importance, few studies in the literature targeted scene and most of them focused on agent-agent interaction. Moreover, accurate annotated dataset rich in agent-scene interaction is rare. We propose another sub-challenge to address agent-scene interaction. Researchers have to study how scene constraint can be added to the models in a generalizable way. We provide a new EPFL-roundabout dataset which consists of 4 roundabouts rich in vehicle-scene interactions. We hope this challenge leads to progress in the agent-scene interaction problem which will advance current research on trajectory prediction.
